My last posts depend on each other, and should be read in sequence. Articles not in bold are optionnal.


  • Assignments Statement Considered Harmful. Pervasive mutable sate make programs more difficult to deal with. While this is old news, most programmers don't know or don't care. It's a pitty, because simple remedies exist.
  • Defining Syntactic Sugar. I sometimes hear arguments about what is or is not syntactic sugar. This is an attempt at defining it.
  • Class-based Programming as Syntactic Sugar. Most of the time, "Object Oriented" actually mean classes. Classes are nothing more than syntactic sugar, at least in statically typed languages such as Java and C++. Sugar is not useless, but seeeing past it helps us understand classes better.
  • Taboo "OO". The term "OO" is so overloaded that we should stop using it. Better substitutes are "classes" and "prototypes".
  • How Class based Programming Sucks. Classes are vastly sub-optional. Functional programming is far better.
